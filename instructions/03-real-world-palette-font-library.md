# Real-World Color Palette + Font Pairing Reference
### Seed library for "Harmonize" — grounded in actual brands & published design guides, not invented

Each entry: hex codes by **role**, a font pairing (heading/body), and why it works. Where a brand's real font is proprietary (can't be redistributed), a **free Google Fonts alternative** with a similar character is given — flagged clearly. Use this list directly as the MVP's curated combo library (see Requirements Doc, FR1).

---

## 1. Trustworthy / SaaS / Tech

**Inspired by: Webflow's site, "Modern SaaS Blue" pattern**
- Primary: `#146EF5` (blue) · Accent: orange (CTA pop) · Background: `#FFFFFF` · Text: near-black
- Heading: **Inter** (Bold/SemiBold) · Body: **Inter** (Regular) — one typeface, varied weights
- Why it works: a high-saturation primary with a contrasting secondary and a white or black base is a recurring pattern in modern site design, and Webflow specifically pairs deep blue against white with orange accents. Blue reads as trustworthy and stable; a single-family weight system avoids visual noise.
- Best for: SaaS, dev tools, fintech.

**Inspired by: Stripe**
- Primary: deep slate/black · Background: white · Accent: violet/blue used sparingly
- Heading: **Sora** or **Plus Jakarta Sans** (free alternative — Stripe's actual GT Walsheim is licensed) · Body: **Inter**
- Why it works: Stripe's geometric sans-serif has a sharp structure and clean precision that reflects its developer-first, reliability-focused identity — a geometric sans paired with a neutral body keeps the combo feeling precise rather than playful.
- Best for: developer tools, B2B infrastructure, payments/fintech.

## 2. Energetic / Youthful / Media & Music

**Inspired by: Spotify**
- Primary: vivid green or brand-bright accent · Background: near-black or white (Spotify uses both) · Text: high-contrast neutral
- Heading: **Poppins** or **Quicksand** (free alternatives — Spotify's real fonts are the licensed Circular/Gotham) · Body: **Inter**
- Why it works: Spotify uses the same geometric sans-serif across its entire app, website, ads, and motion graphics, producing instant familiarity, and the typeface gives the brand a youthful energy that pairs well with vibrant branding. A rounded geometric sans + a single saturated accent reads "energetic but modern," not chaotic.
- Best for: music/media, youth-oriented consumer apps, entertainment.

**Inspired by: Mailchimp**
- Primary: `#FFE01B` (yellow) · Background/Text: `#241C15` (near-black)
- Heading: **Poppins** (Bold) · Body: **Work Sans**
- Why it works: Mailchimp pairs a bold yellow against a near-black — extremely high contrast and very high recognizability; the warmth of yellow keeps it from feeling cold despite the dark base.
- Best for: marketing/email tools, playful B2B, creator tools.

## 3. Warm / Approachable / Community

**Inspired by: Airbnb**
- Primary: warm coral/red accent · Background: white/off-white · Secondary: warm neutral gray
- Heading: **Nunito Sans** or **Work Sans** (free alternatives — Airbnb's real font is the proprietary Cereal, descended from Circular/Avenir) · Body: **Nunito Sans**
- Why it works: Airbnb's typeface was designed to feel friendly yet professional, balancing trust with approachability, and became the connective thread across its global identity. Rounded letterforms + warm accent on neutral background = approachable without losing legitimacy.
- Best for: marketplaces, community platforms, travel/hospitality.

**Inspired by: Tesori Design's "Florist/Interior Design" palette**
- Primary text: Slate (dark neutral) · Accent: Rose (dusty pink) · Background: warm neutral
- Heading: **Libre Baskerville** · Body: **Open Sans**
- Why it works: a serif header paired with a simple sans body keeps the combo elegant yet easy to read, with the serif adding refinement and the neutrals balancing the palette so it doesn't lean too pink.
- Best for: florists, interior design, boutique/lifestyle services.

## 4. Minimal / Editorial / Productivity

**Inspired by: Figma, Notion, Linear — "controlled whitespace" pattern**
- Background: `#F8F8F8` or `#FFFFFF` · Secondary: light gray `#E5E5E5` · Accent: single restrained color (often near-black `#111111` or navy `#1A2B4A`)
- Heading: **Inter** (SemiBold) · Body: **Inter** (Regular) — or swap heading to **Source Serif 4** for a "modern serif, intelligent" feel (per Notion's documented direction)
- Why it works: this whitespace-led pattern lets typography and product content carry full visual weight without competing with the background color, which is itself doing real work rather than sitting empty.
- Best for: productivity tools, dev tools, portfolios, documentation sites.

## 5. Elegant / Luxury / Editorial Fashion

**Inspired by: Vogue**
- Background: white or deep black (editorial contrast) · Text: black or white · Accent: gold or deep burgundy
- Heading: **Libre Baskerville** or **Playfair Display** · Body: **Lato** or **Source Sans 3**
- Why it works: Baskerville's crisp, elegant qualities suit sophisticated, high-fashion material and lend an aura of legitimacy and timeless style. A classical serif heading against a clean sans body is the most reliable "premium editorial" formula.
- Best for: fashion, beauty, premium editorial/publishing.

## 6. Earthy / Natural / Wellness

**Inspired by: 2026 "grounded" trend (home services, healthcare, wellness brands)**
- Primary: warm brown/clay red · Secondary: forest green · Accent: deep teal · Background: warm off-white
- Heading: **Fraunces** (warm, slightly organic serif) · Body: **Karla** or **Mulish**
- Why it works: warm browns, clay reds, forest greens, and deep teals are increasingly common in home services, healthcare, and professional brands because they signal stability, reliability, and real-world grounding — industries where trust matters benefit from this palette doing quiet, unflashy work.
- Best for: wellness, healthcare, home services, sustainability brands.

## 7. Professional / Financial Trust

**Inspired by: Tesori Design's "Financial Advisor" palette**
- Primary text: Slate · Accent: muted Rose (used sparingly) · Background: warm neutral
- Heading: **Libre Baskerville** · Body: **Open Sans**
- Why it works: this combo is explicitly documented as professional yet approachable, a great fit for financial advisors, accounting firms, and consultants seeking to convey trust and expertise — same mechanism as the florist palette above (serif heading + sans body) but on cooler, more restrained neutrals.
- Best for: finance, accounting, consulting, legal.

## 8. Bold / Playful / Community & Events

**Inspired by: "Flowfest" community-event site (via Webflow design blog)**
- Background: warm cream · Accents: peach, coral, mustard, honey (used as stripe/accent shapes, not full backgrounds)
- Heading: a bubbly display font with thick outlines (free alternative: **Baloo 2** or **Fredoka**) · Body: **Quicksand**
- Why it works: the combination of warm, muted colors and rounded display typefaces signals approachability and inclusivity, working equally well for education or family services while still feeling energetic.
- Best for: community events, education, family/kids brands.

## 9. Corporate Modern / B2B Consulting

**Inspired by: "Saxum" B2B communications site (via Webflow design blog)**
- Primary: deep navy · Secondary: complementary grays · Background: clean white
- Heading: **Sora** or **Plus Jakarta Sans** (free alternatives — original uses Gordita) — Bold for headlines, Regular for body, single family throughout
- Why it works: the deep navy and soft gray palette creates a trustworthy, stable feel while the clean, modern sans-serif signals competence and reliability — exactly what B2B clients evaluate when choosing a communications partner, and using one font family across varied weights maintains sophistication without visual complexity.
- Best for: B2B services, consulting, communications/PR agencies.

---

## 10. Dark Mode / Premium Tech

**Inspired by: 2026 "soft dark mode" trend**
- Background: charcoal or warm off-black (not pure `#000000`) · Text: off-white · Accent: one vivid color used sparingly (electric blue, bright orange, or magenta)
- Heading: **Space Grotesk** · Body: **Inter**
- Why it works: pure black is fading out in favor of softer dark palettes built around charcoal, deep navy, and warm off-blacks, which feel easier on the eyes and more premium, especially for long sessions and mobile use; a single vivid accent against that softer dark base reads as intentional rather than harsh.
- Best for: developer tools, premium tech products, portfolio sites.

## Cross-Cutting Rules Extracted From the Research (use these as the app's matching logic)

1. **60-30-10 ratio:** dominant/background color ~60%, secondary/surface ~30%, accent ~10% — applies regardless of mood category.
2. **One typeface family, varied weights** is the safest "looks intentional" formula (Saxum, Spotify, Stripe all do this) — multi-typeface pairings (serif heading + sans body) are reserved for when you want a deliberate formality/elegance signal (Vogue, Tesori finance/florist palettes).
3. **Warm palettes pair with rounder, friendlier type** (Flowfest, Airbnb); **cool/neutral palettes pair with geometric or classical type** (Stripe, Webflow, Vogue) — this warmth-matching is the actual "why" the app should surface, not just a rule sitting in a doc.
4. **Contrast first, mood second:** every example above keeps text-background contrast high even when the accent palette is playful or muted — accessibility and personality aren't in tension in any of these real examples.
5. **Proprietary brand fonts almost always have a "geometric/humanist sans" or "classical serif" free equivalent** — Inter/Sora/Plus Jakarta Sans cover most geometric-sans needs; Libre Baskerville/Playfair Display cover most classical-serif needs; Quicksand/Baloo 2/Fredoka cover most "rounded/friendly" needs.
